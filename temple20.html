📜 History

  • The church at Velankanni is known as the Basilica of Our Lady of Good Health, one of the most famous Christian pilgrimage sites in India.
  • Its origins date back to the 16th century, linked to miraculous events associated with Mother Mary (Our Lady).
  • It is believed that Mother Mary appeared to a shepherd boy and later to a lame buttermilk vendor, healing and blessing them.
  • Over time, a small chapel was built, which later grew into the present grand basilica due to increasing devotion.

🔱 Historical background

  • Velankanni became widely known after Portuguese sailors survived a storm and built a church here in gratitude.
  • The shrine developed into a major pilgrimage center, attracting people from across India and abroad.
  • It was officially declared a Basilica by the Vatican, recognizing its importance in global Christianity.
  • The church is often called the “Lourdes of the East”, similar to Sanctuary of Our Lady of Lourdes in France.

🏛️ Architecture

  • ⛪ White-colored tall structure with pointed arches
  • 🏰 High spires (towers) visible from a distance
  • 🪟 Stained glass windows depicting biblical scene
  • 🙏 Spacious prayer halls for thousands of devotees

🙏 Cultural significance

  • Known as a healing shrine, where devotees believe prayers cure illnesses
  • Attracts people from all religions, not just Christians
  • Candles in shapes of body parts (symbolizing healing)
  • Walking on knees as a sign of faith
